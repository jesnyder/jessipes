Maitake Mushroom Rice

A light, umami-forward side dish cooked in the Instant Pot. The maitake mushrooms cook directly with the rice and release their liquid into it, producing a deeply flavored grain without any added stock. Finished with sesame oil and ginger. Serves 2–3 as a side.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup short-grain white rice, rinsed ✓ in stock — use the white rice, not Arborio (too starchy) or Mexican style (pre-seasoned)
  • 100g maitake mushrooms, torn into small pieces ✓ in stock
  • ⅞ cup water (just under 1 cup — mushrooms release liquid as they cook)
  • 1 tbsp liquid aminos ✓ in stock
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated ✓ in stock
  • ¼ tsp white pepper ✓ in stock
  • 1 tsp sesame oil, to finish ✓ in stock
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds, to garnish ✓ in stock

Optional additions: ½ tsp white miso dissolved in the water (deeper umami); ¼ yellow onion, finely diced (sweetness and body).

Method

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs mostly clear — 2–3 washes. This removes surface starch that would make the rice gummy. Drain well.
  2. Combine in the Instant Pot: Add the rinsed rice, torn maitake pieces, water, liquid aminos, ginger, and white pepper. Stir briefly to distribute. The mushrooms can sit on top — they will press down as pressure builds.
  3. Pressure cook: Seal the lid. Set to High Pressure, 3 minutes.
  4. Natural release for 10 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure. Do not skip the natural release — opening immediately causes the rice to expel steam too fast and can turn the surface gluey.
  5. Fluff and fold: Open the lid carefully (away from you). Use a rice paddle or fork to gently fluff the rice and fold the mushrooms through. The mushrooms will have softened and darkened; their liquid will have absorbed into the grain.
  6. Finish: Drizzle the sesame oil over the rice and fold in. Add sesame oil after the heat is off — it is a finishing oil, not a cooking oil, and its flavor degrades with heat. Scatter sesame seeds over the top.
  7. Serve immediately, or keep on the Instant Pot's Keep Warm setting for up to 30 minutes.
On the water ratio: Short-grain white rice in the Instant Pot typically uses a 1:1 water-to-rice ratio. Maitake mushrooms are ~90% water and release liquid as they cook, so the ratio here is reduced to ⅞ cup water. Short-grain rice is more absorbent than long-grain and will take up the mushroom liquid readily. If your rice comes out slightly wet, reduce to ¾ cup next time. If it is slightly dry or catching on the bottom, add 1–2 tbsp more water.
On maitake and vitamin D: Maitake mushrooms exposed to direct sunlight (gill-side up, 15–30 minutes) before use can provide over 500 IU of vitamin D per ½ cup — meaningful given the difficulty of meeting vitamin D needs from food alone. This works with fresh or dried maitake. The UV exposure converts ergosterol in the mushroom to vitamin D2. See the smoothie page for more context on vitamin D gaps.

Variations

  • Miso version: Dissolve ½ tsp white miso in the cooking water before adding. Adds fermented depth; barely detectable as miso but noticeably rounder in flavor. Do not increase miso — it can overpower at higher doses in a confined pressure cooker.
  • With onion: Add ¼ yellow onion, finely diced, with the other ingredients. Onion sweetens slightly and adds body. ✓ in stock
  • With dried ginger: Substitute ⅙ tsp dried ginger for the fresh. Deeper warmth, slightly less bright. Works well in the miso version. ✓ in stock
  • As a base for a meal: Serve with a soft-boiled egg, pan-fried tofu, or roasted beets on top. The rice is lightly seasoned and works as a neutral-but-flavorful base.
  • Brown rice version: High pressure 22 minutes, 15 minutes natural release. Increase water to 1¼ cups — brown rice absorbs more. Nuttier flavor, higher fiber and lower GI.
