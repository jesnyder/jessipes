How to Marinate & Cook Paneer

What is Paneer?

Paneer is a fresh, non-melting Indian cheese made by curdling milk with an acid (lemon juice or vinegar). It's high in protein and calcium, holds its shape under high heat, and takes on flavor readily when marinated — making it ideal for grilling, pan-frying, and curries.

Prep the Paneer

  1. Cut into pieces. Cubes (~1 inch) work well for skewers and curries; slabs or triangles are better for pan-frying.
  2. Score the surface (optional). Light cross-hatching helps the marinade penetrate the dense surface.
  3. Pat dry before cooking. Remove excess marinade so the paneer sears rather than steams.
Unlike tofu, paneer does not need pressing. It is already dense and will not expel water during marinating.

Marinade Options

Tikka Marinade (recommended)
  • 3 tbsp plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp each: cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • Pinch of chili powder or cayenne
  • Salt to taste
Simple Herb Marinade
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh or dried herbs (thyme, oregano, rosemary)
Soy-Ginger Marinade
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp maple syrup
Labneh & Miso Marinade

Middle Eastern-inspired with umami depth. The labneh and kefir coat paneer well and keep it tender; miso adds savoriness without reading as "Asian." Za'atar is best added in two stages — half in the marinade, half sprinkled after cooking.

  • 3 tbsp labneh (or thick strained yogurt)
  • 2 tbsp kefir (to loosen the labneh to a pourable consistency)
  • 1 tbsp white miso paste (or ½ tbsp miso + ½ tbsp liquid aminos — see note below)
  • ½ tbsp liquid aminos or coconut aminos (optional — reduce miso to ½ tbsp if using)
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated or minced
  • 2 tbsp grated onion (about ¼ small onion)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼ tsp white pepper
  • 1½ tsp za'atar, divided (1 tsp in marinade, ½ tsp to finish)
  • Salt to taste (go light — miso and aminos are both salty)
Best cooked: pan-fry or broil. The labneh chars beautifully at high heat, forming a crust similar to tandoor-style cooking. Sprinkle reserved za'atar immediately after cooking while the surface is still hot.

Marinating

  • Place paneer and marinade in a shallow dish or zip-lock bag.
  • Marinate for at least 30 minutes, ideally 2–4 hours in the fridge.
  • Paneer is denser than tofu — longer marinating times yield better flavor penetration.

Cooking Methods

Pan-Fry

Medium-high heat with a little oil or ghee. Cook 2–3 minutes per side until deeply golden. Do not move the paneer until it releases naturally from the pan.

Grill / Skewers

Thread on skewers with vegetables (peppers, onion, tomatoes). Grill 3–4 minutes per side over medium-high heat.

Broil / Oven

Spread on a lined baking sheet. Broil at 450°F (230°C) for 10–15 minutes, flipping once, until charred at the edges.

In a Curry or Sauce

Add marinated, pan-seared paneer cubes near the end of cooking a sauce (last 5–10 minutes). This preserves texture and prevents it from becoming rubbery.
