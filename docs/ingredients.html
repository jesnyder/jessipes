Ingredient Stock Role Cal / 100g Protein / 100g Key Nutrients Notes
Szechuan peppercorns In stock Aromatic / Spice ~300 ~7g Hydroxy-alpha-sanshool (numbing compound), linalool, limonene Not true pepper — dried husks of Zanthoxylum shrub. Active compound sanshool produces numbing-tingling sensation (má) via touch receptors, distinct from chili heat. Toast briefly before grinding to activate aroma. Use sparingly — ¼ tsp is enough to notice the effect.
Paprika In stock Aromatic / Spice 282 14g Vitamin C, beta-carotene (vitamin A precursor), iron, capsanthin (antioxidant) Comes in sweet, smoked, and hot varieties — the type matters significantly. Sweet paprika is mild and fruity; smoked paprika adds a deep, woody, almost meaty depth (useful in vegetarian cooking to add savory complexity). Heat blooms paprika's flavor — add to hot oil briefly before adding liquid. Used in the Tikka marinade and labneh-miso marinade.
Turmeric In stock Aromatic / Spice 354 8g Curcumin (active anti-inflammatory compound), manganese, iron Earthy, slightly bitter, intense yellow-orange color. Curcumin has well-established anti-inflammatory properties but very poor bioavailability on its own — black pepper (piperine) increases absorption by ~2000%. Always combine turmeric with black pepper. Stains everything it touches. Used in Tikka marinade; add to dals, rice, and egg dishes.
Cumin In stock Aromatic / Spice 375 18g Iron (66% DV/tbsp), manganese Earthy, warm. Used in small amounts — a pinch changes the whole dish. Pairs with acid and fermented flavors.
Za'atar In stock Aromatic / Spice ~300 ~10g Calcium, iron, thymol (antioxidant) Blend of thyme, sumac, sesame, salt. Herby, tangy, nutty. Best added at the end or as a finishing sprinkle.
Ginger In stock Aromatic / Spice 80 1.8g Gingerols (anti-inflammatory), manganese Pungent, warming. Bridges Asian and Middle Eastern flavors. Use fresh for heat, dried for warmth.
White pepper In stock Aromatic / Spice 296 10g Manganese, iron Subtler, more floral heat than black pepper. Preferred in light-colored sauces and dairy marinades where black specks are unwanted.
Onion, yellow In stock Aromatic / Spice Vegetable 40 1.1g Quercetin (highest of all onion types), vitamin C, folate, fiber The workhorse aromatic. Pungent raw, sweet and deeply savory when caramelized. Highest quercetin content of any onion — a well-studied anti-inflammatory flavonoid. Best for cooking (soups, sauces, curries, caramelized applications). Sulfur compounds responsible for eye irritation convert to sweet thiosulfinates when heat-treated.
Onion, red In stock Aromatic / Spice Vegetable Acid 40 1.1g Anthocyanins (red-purple pigment, antioxidant), quercetin, vitamin C Milder and slightly sweeter than yellow onion raw. Anthocyanins are distinct from quercetin — both are present, making red onion among the more antioxidant-rich alliums. Best raw or lightly pickled (slice thin, soak in lemon juice or vinegar 10 minutes — sharpness mellows, color brightens). Color fades significantly with prolonged heat. A small amount grated into a marinade or dressing adds pungency without garlic.
Cucumber In stock Vegetable 15 0.7g Vitamin K (6% DV), potassium; ~96% water Mild, cooling, high water content. Primarily a textural and volume ingredient. Salt and drain before use in salads — removes excess water that would dilute dressings. Smashing creates jagged surfaces that hold dressing better than slicing.
Beets In stock Vegetable Sweet 43 1.6g Folate (27% DV), potassium, nitrates Earthy, sweet. Nitrates support blood pressure and endurance. Roasting concentrates sweetness; boiling loses nutrients to water.
Kale In stock Vegetable 49 4.3g Vitamin K (684% DV), vitamin C, calcium, iron One of the most nutrient-dense leafy greens. Bitter raw; massage with oil or blanch to mellow. High oxalates — rotate with spinach.
Celery In stock Vegetable Aromatic / Spice 16 0.7g Vitamin K, folate, potassium Aromatic base vegetable (mirepoix). Very low calorie. High water content adds bulk to soups and salads.
Spinach In stock Vegetable 23 2.9g Vitamin K (460% DV), folate, iron, magnesium Milder than kale. Wilts quickly — good for cooked dishes. High oxalates reduce iron absorption; pair with vitamin C source.
Sweet potato In stock Sweet / Starchy Vegetable 86 1.6g Beta-carotene (Vit A, 284% DV), potassium, fiber Naturally sweet, filling. Excellent for balancing spicy or bitter flavors. Roast for sweetness; steam to preserve nutrients.
Brussels sprouts In stock Vegetable 43 3.4g Vitamin C (142% DV), vitamin K, folate, glucosinolates Bitter, nutty when roasted. Glucosinolates are anti-cancer compounds. High heat (roast, char) transforms bitterness into sweetness.
Miso paste In stock Umami Fermented / Probiotic 199 12g Manganese, copper, B vitamins; ~3700mg sodium/100g Deep savory base. White miso is mildest; red is strongest. Don't boil — destroys probiotics. Pairs well with acid and dairy.
Kimchi In stock Fermented / Probiotic Acid 15 1.1g Vitamin C, probiotics, capsaicin Spicy, sour, funky. A condiment and flavor bomb in one. Add at the end of cooking to preserve live cultures.
Pickles In stock Acid Fermented / Probiotic 11 0.3g Probiotics (if lacto-fermented), sodium Sour, salty, crunchy. Brine is useful as a marinade acid or seasoning liquid. Check labels — vinegar pickles have no live cultures.
Lemon juice In stock Acid 22 0.4g Vitamin C (28% DV/100ml) Bright, clean acid. Brightens virtually any dish. Helps iron absorption from plant foods. Add at the end to preserve flavor.
Balsamic vinegar In stock Acid Sweet 88 0.5g Polyphenols, antioxidants, potassium Sweet-tart, syrupy. Use sparingly in marinades — can dominate and stain. Reduces beautifully as a glaze. Pairs with roasted vegetables.
Ghee In stock Fat / Richness 900 0g Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K); high smoke point (~485°F) Clarified butter — milk solids and water removed, leaving pure butterfat. Higher smoke point than butter, making it suitable for high-heat cooking (searing, roasting). Lactose-free. Rich, nutty flavor; a small amount goes a long way. Good for sautéing aromatics, tempering spices, and finishing dals.
Labneh In stock Fat / Richness Fermented / Probiotic Protein base 167 11g Calcium, probiotics, B12 Strained yogurt — thick, tangy, rich. Excellent marinade base; chars well at high heat. Can be used as a spread or dip.
Kefir In stock Fermented / Probiotic Protein base 60 3.3g Probiotics (30+ strains), B12, calcium, phosphorus Drinkable fermented milk, tangier than yogurt. Thins thick dairy bases like labneh in marinades. Higher probiotic diversity than yogurt.
Soy milk In stock Protein base 54 3.3g Calcium (fortified), vitamin D, isoflavones Highest protein of all plant milks. Neutral flavor. Good for smoothies, sauces, and cooking where dairy is avoided.
Pesto In stock Fat / Richness Umami ~340 ~6g Vitamin K, olive oil monounsaturated fats, basil antioxidants Ready-made flavor base — oil, basil, cheese, nuts. Use as a marinade, sauce, or finishing condiment. High calorie; a little goes far.
Liquid aminos In stock Umami ~40 ~5g ~320mg sodium/½ tbsp; all essential amino acids Soy-derived, gluten-free, milder than soy sauce. Adds savory depth without dominating. Pairs well with miso — use together at reduced quantities.
Honey In stock Sweet 304 0.3g Trace antioxidants, enzymes (raw only), antimicrobial compounds Natural sweetener with a slightly lower GI than table sugar (58 vs 65), but still high — use in small amounts. Raw honey retains enzymes and antioxidants destroyed by pasteurization. Good glaze base and marinade sweetener.
Mango pickle chutney In stock Sweet Acid ~150 ~1g Vitamin C, beta-carotene, sodium (varies by brand) Sweet, sour, spiced. Strong flavor — use as a condiment, glaze, or marinade addition. Good counterpoint to bitter greens and rich cheese.
Black beans, canned In stock Protein base Sweet / Starchy ~130 ~8.9g Fiber (~8.7g/100g), iron (14% DV), folate (32% DV), potassium, resistant starch Pre-cooked and ready to use — rinse well before using to reduce sodium by ~40%. Slightly softer than home-cooked; fine for soups, salads, and quick meals. Good iron source — pair with lemon juice or tomato to improve absorption. Combine with rice or quinoa for a complete amino acid profile.
Beans In stock Protein base Sweet / Starchy ~130 ~8g Fiber (~6g/100g), iron, folate, potassium, resistant starch Values vary by type — black beans, chickpeas, and kidney beans are all in this range. High resistant starch feeds gut bacteria. Pair with vitamin C to improve iron absorption. Combine with a grain for a complete protein.
Lentils In stock Protein base Sweet / Starchy 116 9g Folate (45% DV), iron (18% DV), fiber (7.9g), potassium One of the best plant protein sources. Red lentils dissolve into soups and dal; green/brown hold their shape. Pair with vitamin C to boost iron absorption.
Mushrooms — Maitake In stock Umami Vegetable 31 1.9g Vitamin D (high when UV-exposed), beta-glucans (immune support), copper, B vitamins "Hen of the woods." Rich, earthy umami — one of the most flavorful mushroom varieties. Roast or pan-fry at high heat to char edges. Beta-glucans support immune function.
Banana In stock Sweet / Starchy 89 1.1g Potassium (10% DV), B6 (20% DV), vitamin C, resistant starch (unripe) Natural sweetener in smoothies. Ripeness matters: unripe bananas are higher in resistant starch (prebiotic); ripe bananas have higher sugar and a lower glycemic index effect when combined with fiber.
Chia seeds In stock Fat / Richness Protein base 486 17g ALA omega-3 (~18g/100g), fiber (34g), calcium (63% DV), phosphorus Soak overnight before use — the gel matrix makes nutrients bioavailable and slows gastric emptying. Dry seeds pass largely intact. Shelf-stable for up to 2 years.
Flaxseed oil In stock Fat / Richness 884 0g ALA omega-3 (~53% of fat — highest plant source), vitamin E Never heat — ALA oxidizes rapidly. Add cold after blending. Refrigerate; discard 6–8 weeks after opening. Not interchangeable with whole flaxseed (different bioavailability profile).
Green tea powder (matcha) In stock Aromatic / Spice ~324 ~30g L-theanine (~19mg/tsp), EGCG catechins, caffeine (~35mg/tsp) L-theanine and caffeine act synergistically for calm alertness. EGCG is one of the most studied dietary antioxidants. Use culinary-grade for smoothies. Store away from light and moisture.
Maca powder In stock Aromatic / Spice ~325 ~14g Vitamin C, iron, potassium, glucosinolates Adaptogen root from the Andes. Evidence for energy and hormonal benefits exists but is from small, short studies. Gelatinized form is easier to digest than raw. Safe; hold benefit claims lightly.
Lion's mane powder In stock Umami ~35 ~22g Beta-glucans, hericenones, erinacines Functional mushroom. Hericenones/erinacines may stimulate nerve growth factor (NGF) — promising in animal models; human evidence is preliminary. Choose dual-extract powder (hot water + alcohol) for full compound capture.
Spirulina In stock Protein base 290 57g Phycocyanin (antioxidant), iron (44% DV/100g), beta-carotene, B vitamins One of the most protein-dense foods by weight. Complete amino acid profile. Note: marketed B12 content is not bioavailable — do not use as a B12 source. Strong flavor; start at ½ tsp.
Rice, white (short-grain) In stock Carbohydrate Sweet / Starchy 130 2.7g Manganese; fast-digesting carbohydrate Slightly sticky, soft texture. Absorbs flavors well — good for mushroom rice and Asian-style dishes. High GI and low fiber compared to whole grains. Instant Pot: 1:1 water ratio, High Pressure 3 min, 10 min natural release.
Rice, Arborio In stock Carbohydrate Sweet / Starchy 357 6.7g High amylopectin starch (creates creamy texture), manganese Italian short-grain rice for risotto. High starch content releases slowly during cooking to create a thick, creamy sauce. Requires gradual liquid addition and stirring — not a substitute for regular rice. Instant Pot risotto works but needs a different approach (sauté aromatics first, deglaze, pressure cook 6 min).
Rice, Mexican style In stock Carbohydrate Sweet / Starchy ~150 ~3g Varies by brand; typically includes tomato, cumin, chili Pre-seasoned rice mix. Already flavored — reduce or omit added salt and spices when using. Good as a quick base for bean dishes or alongside eggs. Not appropriate where a neutral rice is needed (mushroom rice, sushi).
Rice, wild In stock Carbohydrate Protein base 101 4g Fiber (1.8g/100g cooked), manganese, magnesium, zinc, folate; notably higher protein than white rice Not true rice — a North American aquatic grass seed (Zizania). Nutty, chewy texture; dark color. Higher protein and more micronutrients than white rice. Long cook time: stovetop 45–60 min; Instant Pot High Pressure 25 min, 10 min natural release. Often blended with other rices to add texture and nutrition.
Quinoa In stock Carbohydrate Protein base 222 4.4g Complete protein (all 9 essential amino acids — rare for a plant food), iron (8% DV), magnesium (16% DV), fiber, manganese One of the few plant foods with a complete amino acid profile. Rinse thoroughly before cooking — saponins on the surface are bitter. Stovetop: 1:2 ratio, 15 min. Instant Pot: 1:1.25 ratio, High Pressure 1 min, 12 min natural release. Good base for grain bowls; pairs well with lentils, beets, and feta.
Noodles In stock Sweet / Starchy ~130 ~4g Varies by type — egg noodles add protein and B vitamins; rice noodles are gluten-free but lower in protein Protein content varies widely: egg noodles (~4.5g/100g cooked), soba (~6g, high in manganese), rice noodles (~0.9g). Soba is the most nutritious option. All are high GI when cooked soft — cook al dente to lower glycemic response.
Feta cheese In stock Fat / Richness Acid 264 14g Calcium (38% DV), sodium (1116mg/100g — high), phosphorus, B12, probiotics if live cultures present Tangy, salty, crumbly. Use as a finishing cheese rather than a cooking base — heat dulls the flavor. High sodium means it seasons a dish by itself; add salt after tasting. Pairs well with beets, spinach, lemon, and za'atar.
Nutritional yeast In stock Umami Protein base ~280 ~50g B12 (if fortified, ~333% DV per 2 tbsp), zinc (~18% DV per 2 tbsp), B vitamins across the board, complete protein Deactivated yeast with a savory, cheesy umami flavor. One of the best B12 sources for vegetarians when fortified — check label. Also contributes meaningful zinc. Add to sauces, dressings, soups, or smoothies. Distinct from brewer's yeast; not interchangeable.
Wheat germ In stock Protein base 382 27g Zinc (109% DV/100g), folate (70% DV), vitamin E (127% DV), magnesium (57% DV), manganese The nutrient-dense embryo of the wheat kernel — removed during white flour processing. Exceptionally high in zinc and folate; directly addresses two gaps flagged in the morning smoothie. Nutty flavor; add 1–2 tbsp to smoothies, yogurt, or oatmeal. Refrigerate after opening — high fat content means it goes rancid quickly.
Sesame seeds In stock Fat / Richness Aromatic / Spice 573 17g Calcium (88% DV/100g), iron, zinc, sesamol (antioxidant) Nutty, toasty. One of the best plant sources of calcium. Toast in a dry pan before use to intensify flavor. Used as a finishing texture, in za'atar, and as a garnish on Asian dishes.
Sesame oil (toasted) In stock Fat / Richness Aromatic / Spice 884 0g Sesamol, sesamin (antioxidants); oleic and linoleic fatty acids Finishing oil — adds deep, toasty, nutty flavor that is difficult to replicate. Use cold or add at the very end of cooking; it burns easily and degrades with heat. A few drops go a long way. Do not substitute plain (untoasted) sesame oil — entirely different flavor.
Barley In stock Carbohydrate Sweet / Starchy 123 2.3g Beta-glucan fiber (6g/100g cooked), manganese, selenium, B vitamins Pearled barley values shown. Chewy, nutty texture. Beta-glucan fiber has well-established evidence for LDL cholesterol reduction and blood sugar moderation. Lower GI than white rice (~28 vs 64–72). Good in soups, salads, and grain bowls alongside lentils or beans.
Bulgur wheat In stock Carbohydrate Sweet / Starchy 83 3.1g Fiber (4.5g/100g cooked), iron (5% DV), folate, manganese Pre-cooked cracked durum wheat — soak in boiling water 20–30 minutes, no boiling needed. More protein and fiber than white rice; mild, earthy flavor. Base for tabbouleh; good in grain bowls with lentils. Pair with lemon juice to boost iron absorption.
Vinegar, apple cider In stock Acid Fermented / Probiotic 22 0g Acetic acid; "mother" (if unfiltered) contains probiotic cultures and enzymes Slightly fruity, tangy, less sharp than white vinegar. If unfiltered (look for cloudy sediment — the "mother"), contains live cultures. Well-established: modest evidence that 1–2 tbsp before a high-carb meal reduces post-meal blood glucose. Good in dressings, marinades, and quick pickling of onions or beets.
Vinegar, plum (ume) In stock Acid Umami Fermented / Probiotic ~50 ~0.5g Sodium (~700–1000mg per tbsp — use sparingly), citric acid, polyphenols Japanese umeboshi brine — both salty and acidic, not a pure vinegar. Intensely flavored; a few drops are enough. Adds a complex sour-salty-fruity note distinct from any other acid. Use in dressings, rice dishes, or as a condiment. Counts as both your acid and part of your salt — adjust other sodium accordingly.
Vinegar, white In stock Acid 18 0g Acetic acid Sharp, clean, neutral-flavored acid. More aggressive than rice vinegar or lemon juice. Best for quick pickling (cucumbers, onions, beets) where you want acid without color or flavor influence. Use sparingly in dressings — less complex than balsamic or rice vinegar but a reliable preservation acid.
Ginger, dried In stock Aromatic / Spice 335 9g Shogaols (more pungent than fresh ginger's gingerols), manganese Drying converts gingerols to shogaols — a deeper, more pungent heat than fresh ginger. Use roughly ⅙ the amount of fresh (1 tsp dried ≈ 6 tsp fresh grated). Fat-soluble compounds infuse well into hot oil without the splattering risk of fresh ginger's water content. Good for spice blends, baked goods, and oil infusions.
Caraway seeds In stock Aromatic / Spice 333 20g Carvone (primary aroma compound), limonene, iron, calcium Warm, anise-like but earthier than star anise or fennel. Classic in rye bread and sauerkraut. Toast lightly before use to intensify aroma. Traditionally used to support digestion. Pairs well with cabbage, beets, lentils, and fermented dishes.
Mustard seeds In stock Aromatic / Spice 469 26g Isothiocyanates (pungent compounds, same family as cruciferous vegetables), selenium, ALA omega-3, calcium Bloom whole seeds in hot oil — they pop and turn nutty and mild. Grinding releases pungent isothiocyanates (same compounds in broccoli and kale). Whole seeds: Indian-style tempering in oil; ground: mustard pastes and marinades. Pairs well with lentils, beets, and curries.
Coconut flakes Out of stock Fat / Richness Sweet 660 6.7g MCT (medium-chain triglycerides), manganese, iron Sweet, tropical, rich. MCTs are digested quickly for energy. Use unsweetened. Good in smoothies, granola, and Southeast Asian dishes.
Peanut butter Out of stock Fat / Richness Protein base 588 25g Niacin (B3), vitamin E, magnesium, monounsaturated fats Highest protein of the nut butters. Pairs with soy/tamari in sauces. Use natural (no added oil/sugar) for savory applications.
Almond butter Out of stock Fat / Richness Protein base 614 21g Vitamin E (45% DV), calcium, magnesium, monounsaturated fats Milder and slightly sweeter than peanut butter. Higher in calcium and vitamin E. Good in smoothies, dressings, and as a spread.
