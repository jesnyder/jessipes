How to Marinate Tofu

Prep the Tofu

  1. Press it first. Tofu holds a lot of water, which blocks absorption. Wrap a block of firm or extra-firm tofu in a clean towel, place something heavy on top, and press for at least 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours).
  2. Cut into pieces. Cubes, slabs, or strips all work — smaller pieces absorb more flavor.
  3. Freeze then thaw (recommended). Freezing tofu expels water and increases protein density per serving, creating a chewier, spongier result that soaks up marinade exceptionally well. Freeze overnight, thaw completely, then press before marinating.

Basic Marinade Formula

Combine these elements:

RoleExamples
Salt / umamiSoy sauce, tamari, miso, liquid aminos
AcidRice vinegar, lemon juice, lime juice
FatSesame oil, olive oil
FlavorGarlic, ginger, chili flakes, smoked paprika
Sweet (optional)Maple syrup, honey, brown sugar
Simple go-to marinade:
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp maple syrup

Marinating

  • Place tofu and marinade in a zip-lock bag or shallow dish.
  • Marinate for at least 30 minutes at room temperature, or several hours to overnight in the fridge.
  • Flip or shake occasionally so all sides are coated.

After Marinating

Save the leftover marinade — you can use it as a glaze, sauce, or baste while cooking.

Cooking methods:

  • Bake: 400°F (200°C) for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway
  • Pan-fry: Medium-high heat with a little oil, 3–4 minutes per side until golden
  • Air-fry: 375°F (190°C) for 15–20 minutes, shaking halfway
  • Grill: On skewers or a grill pan, 3–4 minutes per side
